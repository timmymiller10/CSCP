# Traceability, Accuracy, and Disposition

## Traceability & Configuration Management
These processes ensure product integrity and data accuracy throughout the supply chain.

* **Product Traceability**: The ability to provide a **chain of custody** for a product and its components. It tracks origin, production facility, and distribution points.
    * *Key Benefit*: Allows for **targeted recalls** (recalling only affected items rather than an entire product line).
    * *Compliance*: Essential for "Made in USA" labels, conflict mineral verification, and pharmaceutical safety.

* **Configuration Management**: Managing product data (specifications, versions, and changes) across the lifecycle to ensure everyone is working from the same version.

* **Enabling Tech**: Barcodes, RFID, GPS, Blockchain (for secure, multi-point records), and WMS/TMS systems.

## Inventory Accuracy & Counting Methods
Inventory accuracy is a metric comparing the physical on-hand quantity to the system's recorded balance.

| Feature | Periodic Counting | Cycle Counting |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Frequency** | Once a year (Annual). | Continuous/Cyclic. |
| **Disruption** | High (requires facility shutdown). | Low (no shutdown needed). |
| **Goal** | Financial valuation. | Root cause identification of errors. |
| **Expertise** | Low (often uses temps). | High (trained staff). |

**Cycle Counting Strategy (ABC Analysis)**
Inventory is typically counted based on its importance/value:

* A-Items: Counted most frequently (e.g., Monthly).
* B-Items: Counted moderately (e.g., Quarterly).
* C-Items: Counted least often (e.g., Semiannually).

**Pro Tip**: The best time to count an item is when its inventory is at its lowest or zero (right before a replacement order arrives).

## Product End-of-Life (EOL) & Disposition
EOL management involves planning a product's phase-out to avoid "lumpy" demand or excess obsolete stock.

**EOL Strategy Components**
* **End-of-Sales Schedule**: Usually kept secret until the official announcement to prevent customers from waiting for the new version and leaving old stock unsold.
* **End-of-Service**: Deciding how long to provide repairs and technical support.
* **Lifetime Buy Quantity**: The amount of spare parts a customer must purchase now to maintain a product until the end of its useful life because the parts are being discontinued.

**Disposition Options (Reverse Logistics)**
When inventory is returned or becomes obsolete, management chooses a "disposition" path. **80% of reverse logistics value** is derived from choosing the right option:

1. **Return to Stock**: For unused/original packaging items.

2. **Return to Manufacturer**: Contractual returns or refillables.

3. **Liquidate**: Selling to a secondary "discount" market.

4. **Donate**: Charitable giving for tax write-offs.

5. **Recycle**: Environmentally responsible recovery.

6. **Dispose**: Final stage (landfill or incineration).

## Key Takeaways

* **Inventory Accuracy Metric**: Targets are typically 95% to 99%, depending on the value of the item.
* **Obsolescence Planning**: Requires removing safety stocks, reducing lot sizes, and updating "effectivity dates" in the MRP system as a product nears its end.
* **Configuration Control**: Prevents manufacturing errors by ensuring all parties use current engineering specifications.
